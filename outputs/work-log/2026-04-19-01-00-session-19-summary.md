## Diagnosis

### The plumbing is fine. The strategy is not.

The engine, signals, and backtest framework worked correctly. The problem is the strategy hypothesis does not produce a positive edge on ZN 5m bars over 2010–2026.

**Three distinct problems were found:**

**1. No RTH filter (biggest contributor to noise)**
75.5% of entries (8,025 trades) were during the overnight session (outside 8:20am–3:00pm ET). These generate same-bar entry/exit losses of ~$35–66 (commission + slippage) because the engine's EOD flat fires immediately when a position opens after 3:00pm ET. These trades are not strategy execution — they're implementation noise.

**2. RTH-only performance is also deeply negative**
Even filtering to RTH non-same-bar trades only (2,116 trades):
- Win rate: 11.2%
- Expectancy: -$60.05/trade
- Avg win: $43.66, Avg loss: -$73.20
- Breakeven win rate required: 62.6% — we're at 11.2%

**3. The failure is regime-independent**
Walk-forward confirms: 10 folds, 10 failures, consistent Sharpe of -20 to -23, consistent Calmar of -0.41 to -0.47. This is not a regime problem. It's a hypothesis problem.

**Root cause of the directional failure:**

The zero-cross exit fires when the MACD histogram crosses back above zero. That's a MACD event, not a price event. The price can be anywhere when the histogram crosses zero — in particular, it can be below the entry price while the histogram is still recovering. The strategy enters long (expecting price up) but exits when the histogram recovers regardless of price. The result: the exit fires at a price below entry, while momentum was recovering. That's a loss.

The win rate is 11.2%, not ~50%, because the sequence is:
1. Enter long when histogram is below zero and fading
2. Histogram fades further down first (adverse excursion)
3. Eventually histogram crosses zero back up
4. But price, which led the histogram decline, is still below entry

This is a systematic mis-timing between the momentum signal and the price level.

**Blackout filter edge case (minor)**
6/10,631 trades leaked on event days — all overnight entries (midnight ET) where the signal bar was on event-day eve but the next-bar entry landed in the early AM of the event day. Minor: 0.056% of trades, all small losses. Fix requires checking the entry bar date, not the signal bar date. Not the priority.

## Decisions Made

- The blackout filter architecture is correct and will be reused for any revised strategy
- Do not tune parameters to fix a broken hypothesis — that's curve fitting
- The immediate next step is strategy redesign, not parameter optimization
