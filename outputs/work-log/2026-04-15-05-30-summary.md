## Decisions made

- Zero-cross exit uses the daily bias as a filter (`daily > 0` for long exit, `daily < 0` for short exit) in the current implementation. This creates a trap: if daily bias flips after entry, the exit never fires and the position is held until EOD or stop. This was identified as a secondary issue but not yet fixed — see Next session below.
- NaN stop is the sentinel that distinguishes entry signals from exit-only signals. This is a clean design that lets signal=-1 mean either "short entry" (finite stop) or "close long" (NaN stop) without adding a separate column.

## Backtest findings

Zero-cross exit is structurally broken for this entry timing:
- 53% EOD exits at 0-minute hold: entries firing near close, wasted
- Signal exits (zero-cross): median gross move -0.016 pts, 90% of trades move < 0.05 pts before exit; costs = 0.035 pts → consistently losing
- Stop exits: 5m ATR × 2 ≈ 2-4 ticks, far too tight; 0.1% win rate on stops
- Root cause: "fading streak" entries fire when histogram is already near zero. Zero-cross exit fires 1-2 bars later. No room for price to move between entry and exit.
