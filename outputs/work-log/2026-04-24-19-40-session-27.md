## Decisions made

- **DFA vs R/S**: Spec claimed DFA gives H < 0.45 for AR(1) φ=0.5. This is mathematically wrong — DFA-1 gives H ≈ 0.5 for ANY short-memory stationary process (including positive-φ OU processes), because the integrated series is indistinguishable from a random walk at the window scales used. DFA still solves the original problem: it no longer gives H > 0.55 (TRENDING) for φ=0.5, which R/S did. That was the actual misclassification being fixed.
- **Option A composite fix**: Rather than trying to make Hurst distinguish OU-positive-φ from random walk (impossible with standard DFA-1), the classification logic was fixed so that ADF + OU half-life are the primary gates for TRADEABLE_MR. Hurst TRENDING still blocks (flags INDETERMINATE — ADF/Hurst contradiction worth investigating). Hurst RANDOM_WALK no longer blocks when ADF passes and OU half-life is in range.
- **Composite formula**: `ln(PF) × (1 - DD) × (1 + log₁₀(N / N_min))`. PF log-compressed; DD multiplicative; trade count log₁₀ bonus; hard floor at N < N_min or DD ≥ 100%. Not applying BH to strategy ranking — composite scores are not p-values. BH is for feature-selection p-value tables.
